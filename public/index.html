Mars

Mars, better known as the Red Planet, has been a very captivating planet for scientists due to its similarity and proximity to Earth. It has a thin atmosphere, mostly composed of carbon dioxide, and is home to the tallest volcano in the solar system, Olympus Mons. Mars has a mass of 6.4185 × 10^23 kg, a volume of 1.6318 × 10^11 km³, and a radius of 3389.5 kilometers.

Mercury

Mercury is the smallest planet and the closest to the Sun. Its surface is covered in craters due to frequent collisions with meteors. Despite its proximity to the Sun, Mercury has extreme temperature fluctuations, ranging from -173°C at night to 427°C during the day. It has a mass of 3.285 × 10^23 kg and a radius of 2,439.7 kilometers.

Venus

Venus is often called Earth’s twin because of their similar size and composition, but it is a very hostile place with a thick atmosphere that traps heat, making it the hottest planet in the solar system with surface temperatures of 462°C. Venus rotates in the opposite direction of most planets, and its day is longer than its year. It has a mass of 4.867 × 10^24 kg and a radius of 6,051.8 kilometers.

Earth

Earth is the only planet known to harbor life, with vast oceans and a protective atmosphere that moderates temperatures. About 71% of Earth's surface is covered by water. The planet has a mass of 5.972 × 10^24 kg, a radius of 6,371 kilometers, and orbits the Sun at an average distance of 149.6 million kilometers.

Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in the solar system, known for its Great Red Spot, a massive storm that has raged for centuries. Its atmosphere is mostly hydrogen and helium, and it has 79 moons, including Ganymede, the largest moon in the solar system. Jupiter has a mass of 1.898 × 10^27 kg and a radius of 69,911 kilometers.

Saturn

Saturn is famous for its beautiful ring system, made of ice, rock, and dust. It is a gas giant composed mostly of hydrogen and helium. Saturn has over 80 moons, and one of them, Titan, is larger than Mercury. The planet has a mass of 5.683 × 10^26 kg and a radius of 58,232 kilometers.

Uranus

Uranus is unique in that it rotates on its side, possibly due to a collision with another celestial body. It is an ice giant with a pale blue-green color due to the methane in its atmosphere. Uranus has a mass of 8.681 × 10^25 kg and a radius of 25,362 kilometers.

Neptune

Neptune is the farthest planet from the Sun and is known for its intense blue color. It is a windy ice giant, with the fastest winds in the solar system reaching up to 2,100 km/h. Neptune has 14 known moons and a mass of 1.024 × 10^26 kg with a radius of 24,622 kilometers.
